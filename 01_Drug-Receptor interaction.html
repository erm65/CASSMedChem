Key points

The main concept of Pharmacodynamics is the binding of one protein (P) and its ligand (L)  to form the complex (PL)-->the interaction is reversible and non covalent and is represented as a chemical equilibrium (image)

-The protein is the Drug target: enzymes, metabolites, protein, receptors, ion channel, transporters,DNA..the ligand is the Drug.

-The drug interaction happens in a water environment, when the interaction takes place the water molecules have to be removed

based on the thermodynamic equation.

-Interactions between the protein and the ligand are non-covalent --> semplifications are required to study the intermolecular interactions --> classical mechanics where forces of the interaction between two atoms are related to potential where the potential equation, described by the ionic interaction, is inversely proportional to the distance.

-Hydrophobic effect: considering two polar substances in the unaggregated state, water molecules are highly ordered and the entropy (ΔS) is lower energetically unfavourable. When the hydrophobic aggregation takes place the water population appears less ordered: there is an higher entropy and it results energetically more favourable. According to the thermodynamic equation an increase of entropy induce a negative free Energy (ΔG).

-All the non covalent bonds are weak interactions---> cooperative effect all together create a strong bonding (but reversible)

ionic bond (the strongest non-covalent bond)> ione-dipole> dipole-dipole--> they occurs because there is a charge difference between the two atoms involved in a bond

-Conformational effects: small molecules rarely bind to proteins outside of their preferred minimum energy conformations,
