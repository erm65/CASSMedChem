DRUG INTERACTIONS

DIHYDROPYRIDINES

Drugs able to bind the L-type voltage gated channel:

  • Drugs that exert allosteric modulation (the side wall of the PD, between subunit three and four)

  • Nifedipine ( antagoinist, no chirality so is used as structural reference)
  • Bay-8644 (R-enantiomer is an antagonist; S-enantiomer in an agonist but at high concentartions became an agonist)
  • Amlodipine (antagoinst, pH dependent efficacy)

  • Drugs that directly block the pore domain

  • Benzothiazepines (Diltiazem)
  • Phenylakylamines (Verapamil)


A structure without the ligand is used as reference to track the conformational changes due to the binding of SBK or RBK

  • When SBK enter in the binding site it cause the rotation of the Glutammate 939 that allow the formation of the fundamental hydrogen bond between the side chain of the tyrosine and the ester group of the SBK (point mutations on Tyr1048 reduce the affinity of DHPs for the protein)
  • Differences between enantiomers:RBK:  CF3- substituted phenyl ring of the RBK projects on the hydrofobic pocket formed by Valine 932, Threonine 935 and Phenilalanine 1060; the C3-ester of RBK  is postioned on the hydorofobic pocket mad up of Met1366, Val932, Phe 1060; while in SBK this place is occupied by the more polar nitro group
  • Amlodipine: the ethanolamine group form a hydrogen bond with the -OH of the Serine 1011. The positive charge of the etanholamine group (at the physiological pH of 7,4) confer a different binding kinetics that give to the drug the enhanced potency that allow once-a-day administration. Also the interaction of a phospholipid (PE that appear to directly coordinate the amlodipine (the phosphate of the lipid interact with the ethanolamine) but is still unclear how a phospholipid can enter the pore domain in an intact membrane (so there are doubts that this interaction happen also in vivo).
